About GAIA

GAIA (Global Adaptive Intelligence for the Anthropocene) is a next-generation planetary intelligence platform designed to monitor, simulate, and guide restoration of Earth's ecosystems using real-time satellite data, AI analysis, and sensor networks.

🎯 Our Mission

The Anthropocene demands a new form of planetary stewardship. GAIA exists to give scientists, policymakers, and restoration practitioners a unified command center — turning the complexity of Earth's systems into actionable intelligence. Every metric, every prediction, every simulation is in service of one goal: a healthier planet.

✦ Technology Stack
🧠

AI & Machine Learning

Neural networks trained on 40 years of satellite imagery and climate data to detect ecosystem stress, predict climate events, and generate restoration models.

🛰️

Satellite Data

Multi-spectral analysis from ESA Sentinel, NASA MODIS, and commercial SAR satellites providing 6-hour global coverage updates.

📡

IoT Sensor Networks

10,000+ ground-truth sensors measuring soil chemistry, moisture, microbial activity, and biodiversity across 87 biomes globally.

🌡️

Climate Science

Integrated climate models from IPCC data pipelines, oceanographic monitoring, and atmospheric CO₂ measurement networks.

⚙️ How It Works

GAIA ingests data streams from satellites, IoT sensors, and citizen science reports. AI models process this data to compute ecosystem health scores, identify risk hotspots, and run restoration simulations. The Digital Twin layer creates a real-time virtual model of planetary systems that updates every 6 hours. Users can interact with this twin to explore scenarios, log field data, submit climate reports, and simulate the impact of restoration efforts — all stored in a persistent data layer accessible across sessions.
